What is Personal Development?

Personal development is the process of understanding and improving five aspects of the self which are:

  1. Mental
  2. Physical
  3. Spiritual
  4. Emotional
  5. Social

It is the lifelong journey of discovering a person's capabilities and potential, building up human captial, and enhancing the quality of life.

Self-Development Plan

A personal development plan is a guideline for your life and your future success. When you create a personal development plan, you start to define the kind of person you want to be, skills you want to have, and accomplishments you want to achieve. And then you map out your long-term and short-term goals to realize these aspirations and set timelines for when you want to reach them. When you practice self-discipline by setting a goal, making a plan, and working on it every day, you will see personal development and progress within your career.
Creating a plan for personal development helps you get a better sense of control over your life and will make you better prepared for whatever comes your way. Consider these benefits to having a personal development plan:

  • Turn an idea into reality
  • Thoughtout and logical action plan
  • Improve time management
  • Progress tracking system
  • Reduce stress and improve self-confidence
  • Exercise
Developing personal development goals for work can make the difference between success and failure. Creating a self-growth plan for your personal life can mean the difference between life-long happiness and regret.

Setting Goals

Start off by asking yourself the following questions:

  • What do I want to make out of my life?
  • What are my goals and ambitions?
  • What is currently standing in my way of achieving these goals?
Focus on are having a specific outcome that you are constantly working towards, planning and paving a path towards achieving it, being mindful of the obstacles, and understanding the bigger motivation behind your actions.
After answering those questions, focus on what type of outcome you are working towards. Plan and pave out a path towards achieving your outcome, be mindful of any challenges you face and understanding the reasoning behind your actions. When you develop a personal development plan, ensure that it is a SMART goal:
  • Specific: Detailed and clear.
  • Measurable: Set milestones.
  • Achievable: Challenging yet can realistically
  • Relevant: Align your goals with your plan
  • Time-Bound: Pick an end date
